"There were cafes selling croissants with apricot jams, mall-like areas glowing with chocolates wrapped in gold foils and “I heart Berlin” refrigerator magnets, and baristas with hair back in long, rope-like braids serving German beers in warm glasses. But there were no power outlets to be found." -- Thoughts from the SFX Airport, Day 1

"To put it lightly, cross walks are taken very seriously. It can be an empty road, in the middle of the night, so quiet and so still that one could take a nap in the middle of the street. Yet Germans will not cross the street until they see the red figure turn into a green figure. It is nonnegotiable." -- Thoughts while walking

Waiting at cross walks might have been a little bit annoying, but the rule-following ways of the Germans did grow on me. Mostly because you didn't have to think -- you just cross when you're told. And isn't that what good user experience is all about?
